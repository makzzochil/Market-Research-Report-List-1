<p><p>The artificial lumbar disc market is divided into two primary types based on material: biopolymer and metal. Biopolymer materials, often derived from natural sources, offer flexibility and biocompatibility, promoting integration with surrounding tissues. They aim to reduce wear and minimize complications. In contrast, metal materials, typically made from titanium or cobalt-chromium alloys, provide strength and durability, ensuring long-term stability and support. Both types are designed to alleviate pain and restore mobility in patients suffering from lumbar disc issues.</p></p>
