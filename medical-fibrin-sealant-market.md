<p><p>The medical fibrin sealant market is primarily divided into two types based on the source of fibrinogen: pig source and human source. Pig source fibrin sealants are derived from porcine tissues, typically offering lower costs and wide availability, making them popular in surgical applications. Human source fibrin sealants, obtained from donated human plasma, are favored for their biocompatibility and reduced risk of zoonotic disease transmission. Both types serve essential roles in wound closure, tissue adhesion, and surgical hemostasis.</p></p>
