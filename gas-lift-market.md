<p><p>The Gas Lift market comprises two primary types: Continuous Flow and Intermittent Flow. Continuous Flow involves a steady injection of gas to maintain constant production rates from oil wells, optimizing extraction and enhancing efficiency. In contrast, Intermittent Flow involves pulsed gas injection, suitable for wells with varying production conditions, allowing for flexibility in management and potential cost savings. Both methods aim to boost oil recovery but cater to different operational needs and field characteristics within the gas lift system.</p></p>
